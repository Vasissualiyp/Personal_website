Hello!

I'm Vasilii Pustovoit, currently navigating the vast and fascinating field of astrophysics. PhD Candidate at Canadian Institute of Theoretical Astrophysics (CITA), based at the University of Toronto (UofT).

My supervisors are: Dick Bond, Norm Murray, Chris Thompson.

My Research

I primarily focus on high-redshift simulations, using the GIZMO code to explore the early Universe, particularly the formation of Population III stars and intermediate-mass black holes. We are hoping in the near future to obtain Initial Mass Function (IMF) for Pop III stars from simulations of hydrodynamics with accretion in the early Universe. These simulations are essential for understanding the fabric of our cosmos in its infancy.

Programming

In addition to my astrophysics projects, I do a lot of coding. I frequently develop software tools to support my research, employing languages like C, Bash, Lua and Python to solve complex problems or analyze data. My preference for operating systems has historically been Arch, but recently I became very fond of NixOS.

Other passions
